Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 29, 2026 · Last updated: May 29, 2026
The short version: Remember Project is built to protect your family's most private memories. We store only what's necessary to preserve your recordings and make them accessible to your family. We never sell your data. We never share your recordings with anyone outside your family vault. Your recordings are never analyzed, transcribed, or used to train AI models. Your data is yours — always exportable, always deletable.

1. Who we are

Remember Project ("we," "us," or "our") is an iOS application developed and operated by Michael Rivera, based in Texas, United States. You can reach us at support@rememberproject.app.

2. What information we collect

  • Account information: Email address and display name, used for authentication and family vault access.
  • Family vault data: Subject names, birth years, and optional notes you add about the people you are recording.
  • Questions: Text questions submitted by family members, including category and who submitted them.
  • Audio recordings: Voice recordings captured in-app and stored in your family's private vault. These are the core of the service.
  • Reactions: Text responses left by family members on individual recordings.
  • Photos: Optional photos attached to questions or recordings as memory triggers or context.
  • Activity data: Records of who added questions, recordings, and reactions within your family vault — visible only to members of that vault.
  • Crash reports: Anonymous crash data via Firebase Crashlytics to improve app stability.

3. What we never collect

  • Your location at any time
  • Photos of your face or biometric data of any kind
  • Messages outside the app's reaction feature
  • Social media profiles or third-party account data
  • Payment information (handled entirely by Apple)
  • Any data from people being recorded — subjects do not need accounts and we collect nothing about them beyond what you voluntarily enter
  • Sensitive personal information such as Social Security numbers, government-issued ID, or financial account data

4. How we use your information

  • To operate the app: Store and deliver your family's recordings, questions, reactions, and photos securely within your family vault.
  • To improve the app: Anonymous, aggregate crash analytics only. No behavioral tracking, no individual-level analytics.
  • To communicate with you: Transactional emails only — password reset, account confirmation, and family invites you explicitly trigger. No marketing emails without your explicit consent.

5. We do not sell or share your personal information

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal information or your family's recordings with any third party for any commercial purpose. Ever.

Your recordings, questions, reactions, and photos are visible only to members of your family vault — people you explicitly invited or accepted an invitation from.

6. Family vault isolation

Each family vault is completely isolated. Members can only access data from vaults they belong to. Row-level security policies enforce this at the database level — it is not possible for one family to access another family's data even with a valid account.

If you belong to multiple family vaults, each is a separate, isolated environment. Your activity in one vault is not visible to members of another.

7. Audio recordings — special protections

Audio recordings are the most sensitive data we store. We treat them accordingly.

  • All recordings are stored in private, access-controlled storage buckets.
  • Access URLs are signed and time-limited — they expire and cannot be shared or accessed outside the app.
  • Recordings are not analyzed, transcribed, or used for advertising, profiling, or AI training purposes.
  • Recordings are not used to train AI models or for any commercial purpose beyond playback within your family vault.
  • Recordings are encrypted at rest and in transit.

8. Data retention and deletion

  • Recordings, questions, reactions, photos: Retained for the life of your account unless you delete them individually or delete your account.
  • Account deletion: You may delete your account at any time from Settings → Delete My Data. Deletion removes your account, your submitted questions, your reactions, and any recordings you captured. Subjects you created and their associated recordings are also removed. This cannot be undone.
  • Export before deletion: We strongly recommend exporting your family's recordings before deleting your account. See Section 10.
  • After deletion: Deleted data may remain in encrypted backups for a limited retention period before permanent deletion. Crash logs and anonymized technical records may be retained as required for legal compliance or system integrity.

8A. Health information and medical content

Remember Project is not a healthcare application and is not subject to HIPAA or other healthcare privacy regulations. We are not a covered entity under HIPAA, and our security practices are designed for family archiving — not healthcare data storage.

If you choose to record conversations that include discussion of health conditions, medical treatments, diagnoses, medications, or other sensitive health information, you do so at your own discretion and at your own risk. We have no control over the content of recordings and cannot prevent, detect, or restrict health-related content.

We strongly recommend:

  • Recording only with the full knowledge and consent of all people whose voices are captured
  • Being thoughtful about what sensitive health information is recorded and stored digitally
  • Understanding that even encrypted digital storage carries inherent privacy risks
  • Consulting a healthcare provider or privacy attorney if you have specific concerns about recording medical information

Remember Project applies the same security protections to all recordings regardless of content — but if you require HIPAA-compliant storage for medical records or protected health information, you should use a healthcare-specific service designed for that purpose.

9. Data sharing and service providers

  • Supabase: Backend database and file storage (PostgreSQL + Supabase Storage). Data stored in US West (Oregon). supabase.com/privacy
  • Apple: Push notifications (APNs) and in-app purchases (StoreKit). apple.com/privacy
  • Google Firebase: Crash reporting (Crashlytics). Anonymous data only. firebase.google.com/support/privacy

No other third parties have access to your data.

10. Your right to export

Your recordings belong to you. At any time you may export your family vault as a ZIP file containing all audio recordings, named by question and subject — for example, Dad_What-did-the-kitchen-smell-like.m4a. This export is available from Settings → Export.

Remember Project Plus members may also generate a Memory Book — a formatted PDF of all recordings, questions, reactions, and photos, designed to be printed or shared with family.

We will never hold your data hostage. If you choose to leave the platform, your memories leave with you.

10C. Vault administration and succession

Each family vault must have at least one designated admin. The admin controls who can access the vault, invite new members, and manage subjects and recordings.

We strongly recommend designating at least one co-admin for every family vault. If the sole admin becomes incapacitated, passes away, or loses access to their account, Remember Project cannot unlock, transfer, or grant access to the vault under any circumstances — including at the request of family members, legal representatives, or estate executors.

Remember Project is not a fiduciary and assumes no custodial responsibility for family vault data. We provide a secure tool for family archiving. We do not adjudicate disputes over vault ownership, inheritance, or access rights between family members.

In the event that a vault becomes inaccessible due to loss of admin access:

  • Remember Project will not unlock or bypass vault security for any reason
  • Remember Project will not transfer vault ownership or data to any third party, including estate executors or legal representatives, without a valid court order
  • Vault data will remain stored but inaccessible until it is deleted per our standard data retention schedule or pursuant to a valid legal order

To protect your family's memories, we encourage all admins to designate a co-admin during vault setup and to keep their account credentials accessible to a trusted family member.

10A. Legal disclosures

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, subpoena, court order, or other valid legal process. We may also disclose information when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, protect your safety or the safety of others, investigate fraud, or respond to a government request.

Where legally permitted, we will attempt to notify you before disclosing your information in response to a legal request.

10B. Your responsibility to obtain consent

Recording laws vary by jurisdiction. Some locations require the consent of all parties being recorded before a recording may be made. Users are solely responsible for complying with applicable recording consent laws in their jurisdiction before using Remember Project to record others.

Remember Project does not monitor recording content or enforce consent requirements. By using the app to record another person, you represent that you have obtained any legally required consent.

11. In-app purchases

Subscriptions are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store. We never see, store, or handle your payment information.

12. Children's privacy

Remember Project is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. People being recorded as subjects (such as grandparents or parents) do not create accounts, and we collect no data about them directly beyond what you voluntarily enter.

If you believe a child under 13 has created an account, contact support@rememberproject.app and we will delete the account promptly.

13. Security

We use industry-standard security practices including:

  • Encryption at rest for all database content and file storage
  • Encrypted connections (TLS/HTTPS) for all data in transit
  • Row-level security policies scoped by family vault on all database tables
  • Signed, expiring URLs for all audio and photo file access
  • Security-definer database functions to prevent cross-family data exposure
  • Minimum data collection — we store only what is necessary to operate the service

In the event of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify affected users as required by applicable law.
